About Me

Howdy!

My name's Emily and I am a rising senior at Texas A&M University (Gig'Em!). Originally, I am not from Texas actually. I chose Texas A&M because I love Texas and the Aggie spirit. I am majoring in Computer Science with a minor and focus in cybersecurity. At A&M, I serve as the Cybersecurity Club President and Vice President for our WiCyS Student Chapter.

Outside of school, I enjoy going back to NH to spend time with my family and the outdoors. I also love dogs and hope to adopt one once I graduate!

Quick Facts

Volunteering

When I get the time to, I like to volunteer at the Aggieland Humane Society. Something about being around animals helps de-stress; especially when I've been looking at a computer screen all day.

Web Design

During my first couple months at college, I had learned about hackathons. Ever since then, I try to participate in at least one every year. When I do compete, I put on my programming hat and work on web design for whatever project we are working on. Some of my favorite projects include HackRice9 Occupotty and TAMUhack 2020 Concierge.

Traveling

One of my future goals is to travel the world as much as I can. I was adopted from China and have wanted to go back to learn my heritage. I will be traveling to Singapore as part of a study abroad program this winter and I am super excited! Other spots I'd love to explore or go back to include London, Italy, Greece, and more!

Capture the Flag

Within the realm of cybersecurity, a great way to practice skills are through Capture the Flag (CTF) events. At A&M, we have our own tamuCTF, which I participate in each year. Participating in these short competitions is always a fun time!
